Australia's Bushfire Problem

Act I

The Fire

Australia has always burned. But something fundamental is changing — fires are growing bigger, hotter, and arriving earlier with every passing decade. The data tells a story of acceleration that is difficult to ignore.

Chart 01 — Historical Fire Extent

Total Area Burned Across Australia, 1999–2024

The 2019–20 Black Summer dwarfs all previous seasons on record, burning more than double any prior year. The 2023–24 season eclipsed even that figure, driven by record-breaking heat and drought across northern and western Australia.

Source: ABARES / DAFF Forest Fire Area Data; AFAC Annual Season Reviews

Chart 02 — Seasonal Patterns

When Australia Burns: Monthly Fire Frequency by State

NSW and VIC peak sharply in summer while Queensland burns almost year-round. The fire season window is widening as springs arrive earlier and autumns grow drier.

Source: BOM / AFAC Fire Weather Data

Chart 03 — Climate Drivers

Four Variables, One Story: How Climate Fuels Fire

Each bubble is one fire season. X = temperature anomaly; Y = area burned; size = rainfall deficit; colour = dangerous fire weather days. Black Summer sits in the extreme corner of every dimension simultaneously.

Source: BOM Climate Data / ABARES fire records

Chart 04 — Fire Danger Index

Dangerous Fire Weather Days by State and Month (2000–2024 Average)

The Forest Fire Danger Index combines temperature, humidity, wind speed and drought into a single risk measure. The Northern Territory burns year-round through tropical savanna; southern states face compressed but intensifying summer peaks growing more extreme each decade.

Source: Bureau of Meteorology (BOM) — FFDI records 2000–2024

The Collision
